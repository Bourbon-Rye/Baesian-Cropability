Project Overview

The farmer introduces you their world.

Hello traveller! Welcome to our humble town. Forgive us for we cannot provide you food as we are in a crisis. While food crop production is mainly affected by different natural and physical conditions, these are not the only factors that matters for us, farmers. The invisible hand of the market, often overlooked, has the ability to dictate crop production. As travellers, I hope you dream to help achieve food security and promote sustainable agriculture in their little world.

The farmer asks for your help.

They want to know if market conditions have influence on food crop production in their little world, notably during crises and recessions? You accepted their request. As someone new, you are interested in seeking evidence, and exploring the impact of economic forces to agricultural food production using different statistical analysis and machine learning tools. You also intend to uncover patterns that could aid stabilize the current agricultural crisis. You soon left the town to discover more about this world.
Background

You picked up the latest edition of the newspaper.

After the pandemic-driven recession in 2020, the country is still experiencing economic and food crisis. Despite being known as an agricultural country, the Philippines struggle when it comes to food security. There are even reports of President Marcos' declaring price ceiling for 2023 adding insecurity to farmers. This subsequently adds pressure in producing and selling food crops in the market. There are even cases of vegetables, such as cabbages, being wasted or sold at a loss due to oversupply in the food supply chain. This is food that could be reallocated to those who needs it. With the ever increasing population growth and unpredictable international crises, the capacity to produce food for the population is hampered.

It is time to plan out on how you will organize your data.

You establish the following steps to ensure the quality of the data.
The team gathered most of the dataset from the database maintained by the Philippine Statistical Authority (PSA)

To autonomously gather the data from PSA, the team developed scrappers using Mechanical Soup.

Due to the limitations of the scrapper, the team also developed a program to compile the fragmented datasets into one with the help of Pandas.
For proper format consistency, Year and Months column were properly arranged in chronological order. Additionally, textcases for some elements were fixed.

Converted and validated all ".." and 0 elements to blank representations.

Removed columns such as, "Annual" and "Average", that can be derived from current data to reduce dataset size.

Region-Province relationships were derived and kept into a separate file.
