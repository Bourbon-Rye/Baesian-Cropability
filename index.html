We are the Baesians and this is our project, the Baesian Cropability. The project is an analysis of food crop production in relation to different market conditions, policies, and trends. The team aims to gain insights and new knowledge using different data science techniques.

Overview

Introduction

While food crop production is mainly affected by different natural and physical conditions, these are arguably not the only factors that matters for farmers. The invisible hand of the market, often overlooked, has the ability to dictate crop production.

SDG 2: Zero Hunger

The project aims in helping achieve food security and promote sustainable agriculture in the country.

Problem

Does market conditions have influence on food crop production in the country, notably during crises and recessions?

Solution

The team is interested in seeking evidence, and exploring the impact of economic forces to our agricultural food production using different statistical analysis and machine learning tools. The team also intend to uncover patterns that could aid stabilize the current agricultural crisis.



Background

After the pandemic-driven recession in 2020, the country is still experiencing economic and food crisis
Despite being known as an agricultural country, the Philippines struggle when it comes to food security. There are reports of President Marcos' declaring a price ceiling of 41 pesos per kilogram of regular milled rice for 2023, which subsequently adds insecurity and pressure to farmers in producing and selling food crops in the market. There are even cases of vegetables, such as cabbages, being dumped at roadsides or sold at a loss due to oversupply. This is food that could be reallocated to those who need it. With the ever increasing population growth and unpredictable international crises, the capacity to produce food for the population is hampered.

Research Question

How do market conditions possibly affect food crops productivity?

Null Hypothesis

Economic factors do not affect food crop productivity in the several regions of the country

Alternative Hypothesis

Food crops production in the country are dictated or affected by some economic conditions

Action Plan

Analyze and interpret several economic metrics and their relation to different food crops production

Data Collection

The team sourced the data from the database provided by different government agencies, DFA and PSA.

Data Gathering and Processing

  • The team gathered most of the dataset from the database maintained by the Philippine Statistical Authority (PSA)

  • To autonomously gather the data from PSA, the team developed scrappers using Mechanical Soup.

  • Due to the limitations of the scrapper, the team also developed a program to compile the fragmented datasets into one with the help of Pandas.

  • For proper format consistency, Year and Months column were properly arranged in chronological order. Additionally, textcases for some elements were fixed.

  • Converted and validated all ".." and 0 elements to blank representations.

  • Removed columns such as, "Annual" and "Average", that can be derived from current data to reduce dataset size.

  • Region-Province relationships were derived and kept into a separate file.
