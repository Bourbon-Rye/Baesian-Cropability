We are the Baesians and this is our project, the Baesian Cropability. The project is an analysis of food crop production in relation to different market conditions, policies, and trends. The team aims to gain insights and new knowledge using different data science techniques.

Background

Agriculture plays an important role in the economic development of many countries. Despite being known as an agricultural country, the Philippines struggle when it comes to food security. With the ever increasing population growth and unpredictable international crises, the capacity to produce food for the population is hampered.


While the food crop production is mainly affected by the different physical conditions such as soil quality and weather, these are arguably not the only factors that the farmers consider when it comes to planting crops. The invisible hand of the market, often overlooked, has the ability to dictate crop production in a country.

Research Questions

This led the team to answer the following question:

  • How different types of food crops productivity are affected by market conditions?
  • What policies were implemented that affect both the market and crop productivity?

Problem

Does market conditions have influence on food crop production in the country, notably during crises and recessions?

Solution

The team is interested in seeking evidence and new insights using data science to uncover if there is a relationship between the economic conditions and food crops availability in the country.

Data Collection

The team sourced the data from the database provided by different government agencies, DFA and PSA.

Process

  • The team gathered most of the dataset from the database maintained by the Philippine Statistical Authority (PSA)

  • To autonomously gather the data from PSA, the team developed scrappers using Mechanical Soup.

  • Due to the limitations of the scrapper, the team also developed a program to compile the fragmented datasets into one with the help of Pandas.

Tools

Python 3.11.x, Pandas, Mechanical Soup (inclusive of Beautiful Soup and requests), Numpy, Github, Jupyter Notebook
